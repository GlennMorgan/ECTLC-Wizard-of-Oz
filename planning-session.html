Yezlyn and Jazmin,

As we prepare for our first planning session, I want us to keep the purpose and setting of this production clear. This will be a participant-led ECTLC production, planned and carried out primarily by the men, women, and families living here.

We are not trying to create a professional theater production. The goal is to give participants an opportunity to work together, use their talents, try something new, and create an event the entire ECTLC community can enjoy and be proud of.

Staff may provide approval, guidance, access, and support, but participants should take ownership of the planning, rehearsals, acting, music, costumes, sets, technology, and event operations.

Goal of the Planning Session: By the end of the meeting, we need enough information to make a clear announcement at the combined house meeting.

Everyone hearing the announcement should understand:

  • What we are doing
  • Who can participate
  • What types of help are needed
  • What the commitment will be
  • How and when to sign up
  • When the first meeting or rehearsal will take place

Proposed Planning Session Structure

1. Agree on the Vision

We should begin by agreeing on what success looks like.

  • Is this primarily a community-building activity, a performance, or both?
  • Are we attempting the full musical or a shorter ECTLC version?
  • How long should the final production be?
  • Will the production include acting, singing, dancing, narration, or a combination?
  • Will the audience be limited to ECTLC participants and staff, or will families and outside guests be invited?
  • Is the July 17 performance date firm?

Given the short preparation period and the fact that participants have work, classes, appointments, family responsibilities, and program requirements, I believe we should seriously consider a shortened production that we can do well.

2. Identify Participant Leadership

We need to determine who will take responsibility for each part of the production.

  • Overall production coordinator
  • Director
  • Assistant director or stage manager
  • Music coordinator
  • Rehearsal coordinator
  • Cast and sign-up coordinator
  • Costumes and makeup
  • Sets and props
  • Sound, microphones, and projection
  • Children and family participation
  • Front-of-house, seating, and ushers
  • Setup and cleanup

One person does not need to do everything. The goal is to divide the work among participants based on interest, experience, and reliability.

We also need to decide:

  • Who will make final decisions when the group disagrees?
  • Who will maintain the schedule?
  • Who will communicate rehearsal changes?
  • Who will report needs or problems to staff?
  • Who will be the main contact at the Front Lobby?

3. Decide How People Can Participate

The announcement should make clear that this is not only for actors or singers.

  • Speaking roles
  • Singing roles
  • Ensemble or chorus
  • Dancing or movement
  • Narration
  • Stage crew
  • Props
  • Costumes
  • Makeup
  • Set construction
  • Sound and audiovisual support
  • Ushering
  • Programs and signs
  • Photography or video
  • Setup and cleanup

Questions to answer:

  • Does everyone need to audition?
  • Can people join the ensemble without auditioning?
  • Can someone volunteer only for backstage work?
  • Can staff participate?
  • What ages of children may participate?
  • Will children need a parent or approved adult present?
  • Who will decide which participant receives each role?
  • Will major roles have backups or understudies?
Shelter-specific planning consideration: We should avoid building the entire production around one person. Participants may obtain employment, change schedules, leave the program, become unavailable, or have other responsibilities. Major roles and essential production duties should have backup plans.

4. Establish the Commitment

Before people sign up, they need to understand what will be expected.

  • Rehearsal days and times
  • First cast meeting or table read
  • How many rehearsals will occur each week
  • Which rehearsals will be mandatory
  • Technical rehearsal date
  • Dress rehearsal date
  • Expected arrival time on performance day
  • How missed rehearsals will be handled
  • Whether scripts may leave the rehearsal area
  • How cast members will receive schedule updates

We also need to work around:

  • Required classes
  • Church and program activities
  • Work schedules
  • Appointments
  • Curfews
  • Transportation
  • Childcare
  • Program phase requirements

Participation should never be presented as more important than required program responsibilities. The schedule needs to be realistic for the people who live here.

5. Confirm Available Resources

We need a realistic understanding of what we already have and what we still need.

  • Is the Sanctuary confirmed for the performance?
  • When can it be used for rehearsals?
  • What microphones and sound equipment are available?
  • Can backgrounds or lyrics be projected?
  • Who is approved to operate the audiovisual equipment?
  • Where can props, costumes, and sets be stored?
  • What materials can Maintenance, the kitchen, the thrift store, or other departments provide?
  • Is there a budget?
  • Who must approve purchases?
  • Can materials be donated or borrowed?
  • Who will be responsible for returning the Sanctuary to its normal condition?

The production should be designed around available resources. We should not create a plan that depends on expensive costumes, complicated scenery, or equipment we do not have.

6. Prepare the House Meeting Announcement

The announcement should be exciting, but it also needs to be honest and specific.

  1. The name and date of the production
  2. The purpose of the activity
  3. Who may participate
  4. The different cast and volunteer opportunities
  5. The sign-up location
  6. The sign-up deadline
  7. The date and time of the first meeting
  8. The expected commitment
  9. The name of the participant contact person

Possible House Meeting Announcement

ECTLC is preparing a participant-led production of The Wizard of Oz, scheduled for July 17 in the Sanctuary. This production is open to men, women, families, and children of appropriate ages. No previous acting or singing experience is required.

We need actors, singers, ensemble members, stage crew, set builders, costume helpers, sound and audiovisual assistance, ushers, and volunteers for setup and cleanup.

This production will be planned and carried out by ECTLC participants. Anyone who signs up should be prepared to attend rehearsals, practice their assigned material, cooperate with the production team, and follow all normal program requirements.

Sign-ups will be available at the Front Lobby through ____________________.

Our first production meeting will be held on ____________________ at ____________________.
