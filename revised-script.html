ECTLC Participant-Led Musical Production

The Wizard of Oz

Interactive Script, simplified for a shelter-based, participant-led production. Toto is written as a child actor with safe, simple stage business.

Production Note

This version is designed as a 45 to 60 minute community production. It uses simplified staging, a small core cast, optional ensemble support, projected or handmade scenery, and song cues rather than full copyrighted lyrics. The story emphasizes wisdom, heart, courage, friendship, and finding the way home.

Rights and music note: Before performance, the committee should confirm that any songs, backing tracks, character designs, costumes, and promotional language are approved for use. This script avoids printing copyrighted song lyrics and allows the music team to choose approved songs or original short refrains.

Recommended Core Cast

  • Dorothy
  • Toto, child actor
  • Narrator
  • Glinda
  • Wicked Witch
  • Scarecrow
  • Tin Man
  • Lion
  • Wizard / Wizard Voice
  • Aunt Em / Uncle Henry / Miss Gulch
  • Munchkins, Guards, Poppies, Ensemble

Shelter-Specific Safety Rules

  • No real fire, smoke, or unsafe effects.
  • No real dog. Toto is a child actor.
  • Keep Witch scenes theatrical and playful, not threatening.
  • Have understudies for Dorothy, Toto, Witch, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion, and Narrator.
  • Program requirements, classes, work, appointments, and child safety remain priority.

Scene 1: Kansas, The Longing for Home

Setting: A simple Kansas farmhouse area. A chair, small table, and a basket for Toto.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Aunt Em, Uncle Henry, Miss Gulch
Stage Direction
Soft instrumental music begins. Dorothy enters with Toto, who is played by a child actor. Toto may wear simple dog ears, a vest, or carry a small collar prop.
Narrator
Every journey begins somewhere. For Dorothy, it begins in Kansas, where the sky is wide, the work is constant, and her heart is restless.
Dorothy
Come on, Toto. I know everyone is busy, but sometimes I feel like nobody really hears me.
Toto
(Playfully barks or gives a small woof.)
Dorothy
You understand me, do you not? I keep wondering if there is a place where people see more than chores, rules, and problems.
Aunt Em
Dorothy, child, we do hear you. But this farm does not run on dreaming alone.
Uncle Henry
There is work to be done, and storms do not wait until we are ready.
Dorothy
I know. I just wish I could see what is beyond all this.
Miss Gulch
(Entering sharply.) Dorothy Gale, that little dog has caused trouble again.
Toto
(Steps closer to Dorothy, scared but not disruptive.)
Dorothy
Toto did not mean any harm.
Miss Gulch
Trouble is still trouble, whether it means harm or not.
Aunt Em
Miss Gulch, this is not the time.
Narrator
Sometimes the thing that feels small to one person feels enormous to another. And sometimes a storm outside is not the only storm in the room.
Stage Direction
Wind sound begins softly. Lights shift. Cast members or ensemble may cross slowly with gray cloths to suggest the storm.
Uncle Henry
Storm is coming. Everyone inside.
Dorothy
Toto! Stay with me.
Toto
(Runs in a small circle, then back to Dorothy.)
Stage Direction
Music grows. Ensemble creates a simple tornado effect with cloth, motion, sound, and dim lighting. Dorothy and Toto crouch together. Blackout or quick transition.

Scene 2: Arrival in Oz and Munchkinland

Setting: Bright colored flowers, simple signs, and a yellow brick path made from paper, fabric, or painted cardboard.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Glinda, Munchkin 1, Munchkin 2, Munchkin 3, Munchkins, Wicked Witch
Stage Direction
Lights rise. Dorothy and Toto wake in Oz. Ensemble holds flowers or colorful signs.
Dorothy
Toto, this is not Kansas.
Toto
(Looks around, amazed. Gives a curious bark.)
Glinda
Welcome, traveler. Do not be afraid.
Dorothy
Are you real? Or am I still inside the storm?
Glinda
I am Glinda, and this is Munchkinland. You have arrived in a place where the impossible is often just the beginning.
Munchkin 1
She came from the sky!
Munchkin 2
She brought a brave little dog!
Munchkin 3
And look at those shoes!
Toto
(Proud little bark.)
Dorothy
Shoes? These? I did not put these on.
Glinda
They came to you for a reason. Keep them close. They may help you when the road becomes hard.
Munchkins
(Song Cue: Munchkin welcome song, approved music or original short chorus. No copyrighted lyrics required.)
Dorothy
Thank you, but I need to get home.
Glinda
Then you must go to the Emerald City. The Wizard may know the way.
Dorothy
How do I get there?
Glinda
Start with the road in front of you. Follow the yellow bricks, one step at a time.
Stage Direction
A sudden sound. Munchkins freeze or hide. The Wicked Witch enters.
Wicked Witch
Who has been celebrating without inviting me?
Dorothy
I do not want any trouble.
Wicked Witch
Trouble rarely asks permission. Those shoes should be mine.
Glinda
They belong where they have landed.
Wicked Witch
Then keep walking, little girl. The road may look golden, but it will not be easy.
Toto
(Barks bravely, then hides behind Dorothy.)
Wicked Witch
Even the dog has opinions. How charming.
Stage Direction
Wicked Witch exits with a laugh. Munchkins slowly reappear.
Glinda
Remember, Dorothy, the road will test you, but it can also teach you.
Dorothy
Come on, Toto. If home is at the end of this road, then we start walking.
Munchkins
(Song Cue: Yellow road transition. Ensemble may hum, clap, or march rhythmically.)

Scene 3: The Scarecrow and the Need for Wisdom

Setting: A field. A post or chair for Scarecrow. Simple corn or straw props.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow
Narrator
On the road to the Emerald City, Dorothy learns that a journey becomes easier when we stop trying to walk it alone.
Dorothy
Which way now? The road seems to bend in every direction.
Scarecrow
That way is fine. Then again, so is that way.
Toto
(Startled bark.)
Dorothy
Toto, did that scarecrow just talk?
Scarecrow
I did. But I cannot promise it was a smart thing to do.
Dorothy
Why are you up there?
Scarecrow
I am supposed to scare birds, but the birds have started giving me advice.
Dorothy
That sounds discouraging.
Scarecrow
It is. I have straw where a brain should be.
Dorothy
I am going to ask the Wizard to help me get home. Maybe he could help you too.
Scarecrow
A brain from the Wizard? That would be wonderful. I could think before I speak, plan before I panic, and maybe stop arguing with crows.
Toto
(Barks as if approving.)
Dorothy
Then come with us.
Scarecrow
Would you take someone like me?
Dorothy
Yes. Everyone needs help with something.
Stage Direction
Dorothy helps Scarecrow down. Scarecrow stumbles comically but safely.
Scarecrow
I may not have a brain yet, but I know this much. It is better to travel with friends.
Stage Direction
Song Cue: Short wisdom song or reprise. Suggested original refrain: One step, one friend, one road to find, courage in the heart and wisdom in the mind.

Scene 4: The Tin Man and the Need for Heart

Setting: A small wooded area. Two Trees may be played by ensemble members with signs or branches.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Tree 1, Tree 2, Wicked Witch
Stage Direction
Dorothy, Toto, and Scarecrow enter. Trees stand nearby with apples or paper fruit.
Dorothy
Look, apples. Maybe the trees will not mind if we take just one.
Tree 1
Excuse me.
Dorothy
The tree talks too?
Tree 2
In Oz, everyone has something to say.
Scarecrow
I told you this road was educational.
Toto
(Sniffs near the Tin Man, then barks.)
Dorothy
Toto found someone.
Tin Man
Oil. Please. Oil.
Scarecrow
He is rusted solid.
Dorothy
Where is the oil can?
Stage Direction
Dorothy and Scarecrow oil Tin Man joints. Toto may help by pointing, carrying a small prop, or reacting.
Tin Man
Thank you. I can speak again. I can move again. But I am still missing something.
Dorothy
What are you missing?
Tin Man
A heart. I can stand. I can walk. I can make noise when you tap on me. But I want to care deeply, and to know that I am loved.
Scarecrow
We are going to the Wizard. I am asking for a brain, Dorothy is asking for home, and maybe you could ask for a heart.
Tin Man
Would you allow me to join you?
Dorothy
Of course.
Tin Man
Then I will walk with you. Even without a heart, I already feel grateful.
Stage Direction
Wicked Witch appears at side or balcony if available.
Wicked Witch
How sweet. A girl, a dog, a scarecrow, and a walking can of feelings. Keep helping her and you will all regret it.
Toto
(Barks defensively.)
Wicked Witch
You again. Small, loud, and inconvenient.
Dorothy
We are not turning back.
Wicked Witch
Then keep walking. The farther you go, the harder it becomes.
Stage Direction
Witch exits. Group gathers courage.
Tin Man
She is frightening.
Scarecrow
Yes, but she did not stop us.
Dorothy
That has to count for something.
Stage Direction
Song Cue: Short heart song or transition music.

Scene 5: The Lion and the Need for Courage

Setting: A forest. Simple dark cloth, trees, or signs.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion
Narrator
The road now leads into a darker place. Sometimes the fear we hear outside us is really the fear we carry inside us.
Tin Man
This forest is too quiet.
Scarecrow
Quiet can be dangerous. Or peaceful. I am not sure which, because I am still waiting on the brain.
Dorothy
Stay close, Toto.
Toto
(Nervous bark.)
Lion
(Leaping in with theatrical confidence.) Stop right there! Nobody passes through my forest without facing me.
Scarecrow
Your forest?
Lion
That is what I said. I am terrifying.
Tin Man
You are shaking.
Lion
I am not shaking. I am warming up my roar.
Toto
(Barks at Lion.)
Lion
And what is that tiny creature saying?
Dorothy
He is saying you do not scare us.
Lion
Well, that is disappointing.
Dorothy
You are acting mean because you are afraid.
Lion
(Deflating.) Is it that obvious?
Scarecrow
A little.
Tin Man
Very.
Lion
I am supposed to be brave, but I am afraid of noises, shadows, decisions, and sometimes my own tail.
Toto
(Walks near Lion and offers a friendly gesture.)
Lion
The little dog forgives quickly.
Dorothy
We are going to the Wizard. Maybe he can help you find courage.
Lion
Do you think courage can be found?
Dorothy
I think it can be practiced.
Lion
Then I will come. But if anyone asks, I was already coming this way.
Stage Direction
Group links or marches together. Song Cue: Courage rhythm, simple claps, or approved music transition.

Scene 6: The Poppies and Help Along the Way

Setting: A field of poppies. Ensemble may hold red paper flowers. No physical hazards.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion, Glinda, Wicked Witch, Poppies
Narrator
Not every danger looks dangerous. Some obstacles are soft, beautiful, and easy to underestimate.
Wicked Witch
(From side.) They are getting too close. If fear will not stop them, weariness will.
Stage Direction
Poppies enter with slow movement. Dorothy and Lion become sleepy. Keep all movement safe and simple.
Dorothy
These flowers are beautiful. I suddenly feel so tired.
Toto
(Concerned bark, tries to keep Dorothy awake.)
Lion
A short nap sounds brave enough to me.
Scarecrow
No. We have to keep moving.
Tin Man
Dorothy, stay awake.
Dorothy
I am trying.
Stage Direction
Dorothy and Lion sit or kneel safely, not flat on the floor if visibility is poor.
Scarecrow
Help! We need help!
Glinda
(Entering calmly.) Help often comes when someone is willing to ask for it.
Toto
(Runs to Glinda, then back to Dorothy.)
Glinda
Wake, travelers. The road is not finished.
Stage Direction
Glinda waves wand or sprinkles white paper snow. Poppies freeze and exit.
Dorothy
Glinda, you helped us again.
Glinda
I reminded you that the journey continues. You still have to walk it.
Tin Man
Emerald City is ahead.
Lion
Then I am awake. Mostly.
Scarecrow
Forward?
Dorothy
Forward.
Stage Direction
Song Cue: Emerald City transition. Ensemble may create green glow with fabric, signs, or projected image.

Scene 7: Emerald City and the Wizard

Setting: Emerald City gate and Wizard area. A curtain, projected face, or large sign can represent the Wizard.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion, Doorman, Wizard Voice
Stage Direction
Group arrives at a simple Emerald City gate. Doorman appears with clipboard or sign.
Doorman
State your business.
Dorothy
We need to see the Wizard.
Doorman
Everyone says that.
Scarecrow
We came a long way.
Tin Man
We came with purpose.
Lion
We came with nervousness.
Toto
(Barks as if presenting the group.)
Doorman
And the dog came with confidence. Very well. Enter, but speak carefully.
Stage Direction
Lights shift. Wizard Voice is amplified if possible. If no sound system is available, the Wizard may stand behind curtain and speak loudly.
Wizard Voice
Who comes before the great Wizard?
Dorothy
My name is Dorothy. This is Toto. These are my friends. We came to ask for help.
Wizard Voice
One wants wisdom, one wants heart, one wants courage, and one wants home.
Scarecrow
That is correct.
Tin Man
Please, sir.
Lion
No rush, but also quickly if possible.
Wizard Voice
If you want my help, bring me the broom of the Wicked Witch.
Dorothy
You want us to face her?
Wizard Voice
The road has brought you this far. It will not carry you the rest of the way without a test.
Lion
I was afraid he would say something like that.
Tin Man
We go together.
Scarecrow
Together sounds smarter than alone.
Dorothy
Then together it is.
Toto
(Brave bark.)
Stage Direction
Group exits toward Witch Castle.

Scene 8: The Witch Castle

Setting: Darkened area. Witch broom visible. No real fire, smoke, or unsafe effects.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion, Wicked Witch, Guards
Narrator
The hardest part of the journey is often the moment when turning back would be easiest.
Stage Direction
Guards cross or stand near castle entrance. Scarecrow gestures for the group to move quietly.
Scarecrow
I have a plan.
Tin Man
Is it a good plan?
Scarecrow
It is a plan with confidence.
Lion
That is not the same thing.
Dorothy
Quiet. We need to find the broom and get out.
Toto
(Quiet woof, then points or looks toward the broom.)
Wicked Witch
Looking for something?
Stage Direction
Witch enters. She should be theatrical, not genuinely threatening. Keep distance from child actor Toto.
Dorothy
We came for the broom.
Wicked Witch
Then you came for the wrong thing. You should have come for permission to leave.
Tin Man
We are not here to hurt anyone.
Wicked Witch
That makes one of us.
Stage Direction
Witch threatens Scarecrow with a red or orange cloth to represent danger. No flame is used.
Scarecrow
Help! This is not good for straw!
Dorothy
Leave him alone.
Stage Direction
Dorothy grabs a clearly marked bucket or blue cloth labeled WATER. She throws confetti, blue fabric, or pantomimed water toward the Witch.
Wicked Witch
Water? No! I am melting! My wickedness cannot stand this much goodness!
Stage Direction
Witch melts comically and safely behind a cloth or down to a seated position. Guards may react with surprise.
Lion
Did we win?
Tin Man
I think we survived.
Scarecrow
That was a better plan than my plan.
Dorothy
I did not mean to destroy her. I was trying to help my friend.
Toto
(Gentle bark, stays near Dorothy.)
Guards
The Witch is gone. Take the broom.
Dorothy
Thank you. Come on, everyone. Back to the Wizard.
Stage Direction
Song Cue: Victory transition, short and upbeat.

Scene 9: The Truth Behind the Curtain

Setting: Wizard area again. Curtain or sheet is important for reveal.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Scarecrow, Tin Man, Lion, Wizard, Wizard Voice, Glinda
Stage Direction
Group enters with broom.
Dorothy
We brought the broom. Please keep your promise.
Wizard Voice
Come back another day.
Scarecrow
Another day?
Tin Man
But you gave your word.
Lion
I was afraid of this.
Toto
(Notices the curtain. Carefully pulls it or points to it. An adult or stagehand may help if needed.)
Stage Direction
Curtain opens. The Wizard is revealed as an ordinary person.
Dorothy
You are the Wizard?
Wizard
I am afraid so.
Scarecrow
But you sounded enormous.
Wizard
A curtain can make a small person seem very large.
Tin Man
You frightened people.
Wizard
I did. I was afraid they would not listen otherwise.
Dorothy
That does not make it right.
Wizard
No, it does not. But I can still tell you the truth. Scarecrow, wisdom is not only what you know. It is how you use what you learn. You have been thinking the whole way here.
Stage Direction
Wizard gives Scarecrow a simple diploma or scroll.
Wizard
Tin Man, a heart is shown by care, loyalty, and compassion. You have already shown those things.
Stage Direction
Wizard gives Tin Man a heart token.
Wizard
Lion, courage is not the absence of fear. Courage is doing what is right while afraid.
Stage Direction
Wizard gives Lion a medal or ribbon.
Lion
Then I have been courageous and terrified at the same time.
Wizard
That is often how courage feels.
Dorothy
What about me?
Wizard
I cannot give you home from a bag.
Dorothy
Then how do I get back?
Stage Direction
Glinda enters.
Glinda
Dorothy, you have carried more than shoes. You have carried love, loyalty, and the desire to return to what matters.
Dorothy
I had the way home all along?
Glinda
You had the beginning of it. The journey helped you understand it.
Toto
(Soft bark.)
Dorothy
I think Toto knew before I did.
Glinda
Many faithful friends do.
Dorothy
I will miss all of you.
Tin Man
Now I know I have a heart, because saying goodbye hurts.
Lion
I will be brave enough to cry.
Scarecrow
And I will be wise enough not to hide it.
Dorothy
Thank you for walking with me.
Narrator
Dorothy learned that wisdom, heart, courage, and home are not always handed to us. Sometimes they are discovered on the road, through trials, choices, and friends who refuse to let us walk alone.
Glinda
Close your eyes, Dorothy. Hold Toto close. Think of home.
Dorothy
There is no place like home. There is no place like home. There is no place like home.
Stage Direction
Lights shift. Transition music. Dorothy and Toto return to Kansas area.

Scene 10: Home

Setting: Kansas farmhouse area returns. Same chair, table, and basket from Scene 1.
Roles in scene: Narrator, Dorothy, Toto, Aunt Em, Uncle Henry, Full Cast
Stage Direction
Dorothy wakes in Kansas with Toto nearby. Aunt Em and Uncle Henry enter.
Aunt Em
Dorothy, you are safe.
Uncle Henry
That storm gave us a scare.
Dorothy
I saw so much. I met people who needed wisdom, heart, and courage. And somehow they helped me find my way home.
Toto
(Happy bark.)
Aunt Em
Maybe the journey taught you something.
Dorothy
It did. Home is not perfect. But home is where love calls you back, where people help you stand, and where you remember who you are.
Narrator
And so the journey ends where it began, but Dorothy is not the same. The road changed her, and the friends she met gave her gifts she will never forget.
Full Cast
(Final Song Cue: Original ECTLC closing song or approved music. Suggested spoken refrain: One road, one step, one heart, one home.)
Stage Direction
All cast bows. Stage manager cues applause and final exit.

Stage Direction: Wicked Witch exits with a laugh. Munchkins slowly reappear.

Scene 3: The Scarecrow and the Need for Wisdom

Stage Direction: Dorothy helps Scarecrow down. Scarecrow stumbles comically but safely.
Stage Direction: Song Cue: Short wisdom song or reprise. Suggested original refrain: One step, one friend, one road to find, courage in the heart and wisdom in the mind.

Scene 4: The Tin Man and the Need for Heart

Stage Direction: Dorothy, Toto, and Scarecrow enter. Trees stand nearby with apples or paper fruit.
Stage Direction: Dorothy and Scarecrow oil Tin Man joints. Toto may help by pointing, carrying a small prop, or reacting.
Stage Direction: Wicked Witch appears at side or balcony if available.
Stage Direction: Witch exits. Group gathers courage.
Stage Direction: Song Cue: Short heart song or transition music.

Scene 5: The Lion and the Need for Courage

Stage Direction: Group links or marches together. Song Cue: Courage rhythm, simple claps, or approved music transition.

Scene 6: The Poppies and Help Along the Way

Stage Direction: Poppies enter with slow movement. Dorothy and Lion become sleepy. Keep all movement safe and simple.
Stage Direction: Dorothy and Lion sit or kneel safely, not flat on the floor if visibility is poor.
Stage Direction: Glinda waves wand or sprinkles white paper snow. Poppies freeze and exit.
Stage Direction: Song Cue: Emerald City transition. Ensemble may create green glow with fabric, signs, or projected image.

Scene 7: Emerald City and the Wizard

Stage Direction: Group arrives at a simple Emerald City gate. Doorman appears with clipboard or sign.
Stage Direction: Lights shift. Wizard Voice is amplified if possible. If no sound system is available, the Wizard may stand behind curtain and speak loudly.
Stage Direction: Group exits toward Witch Castle.

Scene 8: The Witch Castle

Stage Direction: Guards cross or stand near castle entrance. Scarecrow gestures for the group to move quietly.
Stage Direction: Witch enters. She should be theatrical, not genuinely threatening. Keep distance from child actor Toto.
Stage Direction: Witch threatens Scarecrow with a red or orange cloth to represent danger. No flame is used.
Stage Direction: Dorothy grabs a clearly marked bucket or blue cloth labeled WATER. She throws confetti, blue fabric, or pantomimed water toward the Witch.
Stage Direction: Witch melts comically and safely behind a cloth or down to a seated position. Guards may react with surprise.
Stage Direction: Song Cue: Victory transition, short and upbeat.

Scene 9: The Truth Behind the Curtain

Stage Direction: Group enters with broom.
Stage Direction: Curtain opens. The Wizard is revealed as an ordinary person.
Stage Direction: Wizard gives Scarecrow a simple diploma or scroll.
Stage Direction: Wizard gives Tin Man a heart token.
Stage Direction: Wizard gives Lion a medal or ribbon.
Stage Direction: Glinda enters.
Stage Direction: Lights shift. Transition music. Dorothy and Toto return to Kansas area.

Scene 10: Home

Stage Direction: Dorothy wakes in Kansas with Toto nearby. Aunt Em and Uncle Henry enter.
Stage Direction: All cast bows. Stage manager cues applause and final exit.
